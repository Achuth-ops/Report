Overview
Executive Summary
March was a month of strategic audience optimization. With a total spend of $2,522.54, the campaign generated 80 leads at a cost per lead of $31.53 — a modest increase from February's $28.56 CPL. The rise in CPL was anticipated and directly attributable to two key actions: pausing underperforming audiences that had hit creative fatigue, and consolidating budget into the Broad Audience ad set which continues to efficiently discover new prospects. Importantly, the landing page remains a strong converter at 9.6% (up from 9.0% in February), and the link click-through rate held steady at 1.43%. The campaign foundation is solid — the primary lever for April is fresh creative content.
⚡ Key context for this month: Two ad sets — Email List + Engagers and Interest Targeting — were paused after showing signs of ad fatigue due to repetitive creative exposure. Budget was fully redirected to the Broad Audience ad set. No new creatives were introduced in March, which contributed to the slight CPL increase.

Campaign Story
What Happened in March
1
Email List + Engagers — Paused (Severe Ad Fatigue)
This audience, made up of email list contacts and users who engaged with BKBG's engagement campaign, reached a frequency of 12.17× — meaning the same people were seeing the same ads over 12 times on average. With no new creative to refresh the experience, engagement collapsed: CTR dropped to just 0.49% and only 1 lead was generated despite spending $405.56, resulting in an astronomical CPL of $405. Pausing this ad set was the correct and timely decision.
2
Interest Targeting — Paused (Creative Fatigue)
The Interest Targeting ad set showed similar signs of decline. Without new creative variations, the same ads were repeatedly served to a defined interest-based audience, leading to diminishing engagement and rising CPL. While it still produced 45 leads at $22.75 CPL before being paused, the trend was clear. Budget was redirected to preserve efficiency heading into April.
3
Broad Audience — Now Primary Ad Set (Healthy & Active)
With budget consolidated here, the Broad Audience ad set delivered 34 leads at $32.15 CPL with a healthy frequency of 2.35× — well within the safe range. This audience continuously reaches new people, making it naturally more resilient to creative fatigue than fixed audience segments. It is the right home for budget while new creatives are being developed.

Analysis
Key Highlights & Observations
✅
CTR Held Steady at 1.43%
Despite no new creatives and audience consolidation, the link click-through rate remained exactly at 1.43% — matching February. This confirms the existing ad creative still resonates and is not yet worn out on the Broad Audience, which is seeing it for the first time.
📈
Landing Page Conversion Improved to 9.6%
The landing page converted visitors to leads at 9.6% — up from 9.0% in February. This is well above the industry average of 2–5% and signals that the page messaging and offer are performing strongly and becoming more refined.
⚠️
Email List + Engagers: Extreme Fatigue (12.17× Freq)
A frequency of 12.17× — where the same audience saw the same ad more than 12 times — caused a complete collapse in performance: CTR dropped to 0.49% and CPL hit $405. This audience needs to either rest completely or be re-engaged with entirely new creative before reactivating.
🔄
CPM Rose +16.7% — Broad Audience Effect
Broad audiences on Meta tend to carry slightly higher CPMs as the algorithm works harder to find the right people within a wider pool. This is expected and acceptable — the trade-off is fresher reach and better long-term sustainability compared to exhausted fixed segments.
🎬
New Creatives Are the #1 Lever for April
The data is clear: the lack of new creative in March directly limited performance. Introducing fresh image and video variations in April will allow Interest Targeting to be reactivated, reduce CPL, and unlock the full potential of all three audience segments running simultaneously.
🎯
Broad Audience: Strong Foundation for Scaling
With a healthy frequency of 2.35× and 34 leads at $32.15 CPL, the Broad Audience ad set is performing as expected. As it accumulates more conversion data, Meta's algorithm will become more efficient at finding high-intent prospects — making it a reliable long-term channel.
April Strategy
Recommendations for Next Month
1
Prioritize New Creative Production — Immediately
This is the single most impactful action for April. Fresh images and video creatives will allow all three audience segments to run efficiently. Without new creative, CPL will continue to drift upward as even the Broad Audience eventually experiences fatigue. Aim for at least 3–5 new ad variations (mix of static and video) before relaunching paused ad sets.
2
Reactivate Interest Targeting with New Creatives
Interest Targeting delivered the best CPL in March at $22.75 before being paused. Once new creatives are ready, reactivating this ad set with a fresh rotation should bring CPL back down and add meaningful lead volume alongside the Broad Audience.
3
Rest Email List + Engagers — Relaunch with Fresh Creative Only
The Email List + Engagers audience needs a complete reset. Do not reactivate with any creative that has already been shown to this segment. When relaunching, use entirely new content and set a frequency cap (recommended: pause or rotate when frequency exceeds 4–5×) to prevent the same fatigue pattern from repeating.
4
Build a Retargeting Campaign for Non-Converted LPV Visitors
756 people visited the landing page in March without signing up. A dedicated retargeting ad set with a tailored message — such as a benefit highlight, testimonial, or urgency-based copy — can re-engage this warm audience at very low cost and recover additional leads from already-paid traffic.
5
Test a Lookalike Audience Based on Converters
With 165 total sign-up conversions now tracked across February and March (85 + 80), there is strong pixel data to build a 1% Lookalike Audience from people who completed the sign-up. Testing this alongside Broad Audience targeting can open a high-quality new prospect pool that mirrors your best converters.
